the H!t Indie Game

    Explore a strange world full of colorful friends and foes. Navigate through the vibrant and the mundane in order to uncover a forgotten past. When the time comes, the path you’ve chosen will determine your fate... and perhaps the fate of others as well. OMORI is a video game by OMOCAT. OMOCAT, LLC

    OMOCAT is a L.A.-based indie art studio founded in 2014. The company is made up of a small, but growing, group of friends and creatives that work on OMOCAT's various projects together. In 2020, OMOCAT released the hit indie video game, OMORI, gaining recognition brand that creates quality goods with their own artistic flair. Unique Gameplay

    OMORI’s gameplay stands out because it blends traditional turn-based RPG mechanics with an innovative emotion system that directly affects battles and storytelling. Instead of relying only on status effects like poison or paralysis, characters in OMORI experience emotions such as Happy, Sad, and Angry, each influencing stats and interactions in rock-paper-scissors fashion. This adds a psychological layer where managing feelings becomes just as important as managing health or skills. Beyond combat, the game alternates between a surreal dream world and the real world, encouraging exploration, puzzle-solving, and choices that shape branching storylines and multiple endings. This mix of emotional depth, unconventional mechanics, and narrative consequences makes OMORI’s gameplay a unique experience compared to typical RPGs.


The Synergies of the Characters

    In OMORI, battles use a unique emotion system where characters’ moods interact like a rock-paper-scissors cycle, creating powerful synergies. A character who is Happy gains higher luck and speed but has lower accuracy, and this emotion is strong against Angry opponents. Angry characters hit harder with increased attack but lose defense, and they are strong against Sad foes. Meanwhile, Sad characters boost their defense but lose speed, redirecting some damage from their health (“Heart”) to their mana pool (“Juice"), and they are strong against those who are Happy. Neutral characters have no emotional modifiers, making them stable but less dynamic. Mastering these synergies; Happy > Angry > Sad > Happy — adds depth to combat and encourages players to strategically manipulate emotions for an advantage.


Character Skills

    In OMORI, beginner players can take advantage of simple yet effective skill and emotion combinations to gain an edge in battle. For example, making Aubrey Angry boosts her attack, allowing her heavy-hitting skills to deal extra damage, while turning Kel Happy increases his speed so he can act first with moves like Rebound to quickly chip away at multiple enemies. Meanwhile, Hero can stay Neutral or Sad, focusing on healing and support to keep the team stable, and Omori serves as a versatile attacker with reliable skills like Stab or emotion-based moves to exploit enemy weaknesses. This basic setup, Kel as the speedy opener, Aubrey as the powerhouse, Hero as the supporter, and Omori as the flexible damage dealer, helps beginners understand the synergy between emotions and skills without overcomplicating strategy.
